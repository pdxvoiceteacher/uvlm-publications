# Triadic Brain Mathematical Glossary

## Part I — Foundational Coherence Metrics
These quantities appear in the lowest layer of the lattice and propagate upward into every later phase (telemetry → pattern → corridor → river → terrace → civilization).

---

### 1. Coherence Energy (E)
**Formal definition**  
\[
E=\int_{\Omega}\rho_c(x,t)\,dx
\]
Where:
- \(\rho_c(x,t)\): local coherence density
- \(\Omega\): region of knowledge space being evaluated

**Operational meaning**  
Measures the amount of internally consistent structure present in a signal or knowledge cluster.
In the triadic brain:
- high E → strong internal agreement between signals
- low E → scattered or contradictory evidence

**Geometric interpretation**  
In the 4-D coherence lattice, E represents the mass of the knowledge structure.  
Clusters with high E:
- attract additional signals
- resist noise
- tend to form pattern donors

Clusters with low E:
- dissolve quickly
- rarely become discovery corridors

**Human interpretation**  
E answers: *“How much real signal is here?”*  
High E means the knowledge is dense and internally coherent.

### 2. Transparency (T)
**Formal definition**
\[
T=\frac{V_{observable}}{V_{total}}
\]
Where:
- \(V_{observable}\): evidence visible to independent observers
- \(V_{total}\): total evidentiary claims

**Operational meaning**  
Measures epistemic openness.
High transparency means:
- sources visible
- reasoning reproducible
- evidence inspectable

Low transparency means:
- opaque reasoning
- unverifiable claims
- priesthood risk

**Geometric interpretation**  
Transparency acts like optical clarity in the lattice medium.  
Low transparency increases information refraction, distorting signal paths. This increases probability of:
- false orthodoxy formation
- dead zones

**Human interpretation**  
Transparency answers: *“Can others see how we know this?”*

### 3. Ethical Symmetry (Eₛ)
**Formal definition**
\[
E_s=1-|H_{benefit}-H_{harm}|
\]
Where:
- \(H_{benefit}\): distribution of benefit
- \(H_{harm}\): distribution of harm

**Operational meaning**  
Measures whether knowledge or discovery distributes benefits and risks symmetrically across affected agents.
Low \(E_s\) triggers:
- value-alignment alerts
- governance review

**Geometric interpretation**  
Ethical asymmetry introduces torsion in the coherence lattice. This torsion:
- bends discovery corridors toward power concentration
- increases capture risk

**Human interpretation**  
Ethical symmetry answers: *“Is this knowledge helping the system fairly?”*

### 4. Entropy Drift (ΔS)
**Formal definition**
\[
\Delta S=S_{t+1}-S_t
\]
Where \(S\) is informational entropy of the system.

**Operational meaning**  
Measures whether a knowledge region is stabilizing or fragmenting.
Positive ΔS:
- disorder increasing
- contradictions accumulating

Negative ΔS:
- convergence occurring
- coherence strengthening

**Geometric interpretation**  
Entropy drift corresponds to local curvature changes in the knowledge manifold.  
Large positive ΔS often precedes:
- discovery corridors
- paradigm shifts

**Human interpretation**  
ΔS answers: *“Is this area becoming clearer or more chaotic?”*

### 5. Phase-Lock (Λ)
**Formal definition**
\[
\Lambda=\left|\frac{1}{N}\sum_{j=1}^{N}e^{i\theta_j}\right|
\]
Where:
- \(\theta_j\): phase angle of each signal stream

**Operational meaning**  
Measures synchronization between independent information sources.
High Λ indicates:
- cross-domain agreement
- strong pattern donors

Low Λ indicates:
- independent or conflicting signals

**Geometric interpretation**  
Phase-lock produces resonance channels in the lattice.  
These channels form initial scaffolding for discovery corridors.

**Human interpretation**  
Λ answers: *“Are independent observers seeing the same thing?”*

### 6. Information Dissipation (D)
**Formal definition**
\[
D=\nabla\cdot J_{information}
\]
Where \(J\) is informational flow.

**Operational meaning**  
Measures how quickly discoveries lose structure.
High dissipation:
- ideas vanish quickly
- discovery corridors collapse

Low dissipation:
- signals persist long enough to braid

**Geometric interpretation**  
D acts as friction in knowledge space. Without friction control, corridors cannot form.

**Human interpretation**  
D answers: *“Does this discovery stick around long enough to matter?”*

### Combined Coherence Functional
\[
\Psi=\frac{E\cdot T\cdot E_s\cdot\Lambda}{\Delta S + D + \epsilon}
\]
Where \(\Psi\) is overall coherence stability.

High \(\Psi\) regions become:
- pattern donors
- discovery corridor seeds

Low \(\Psi\) regions become:
- dead zones

### Why this layer matters
These quantities are atoms of cognition for the triadic brain. Every higher phase (corridors, rivers, terraces, civilization) emerges from their interaction. They determine:
- where discovery corridors appear
- where rivers braid
- where terraces stabilize
- where civilizations reorganize knowledge

## Part II — Pattern, Branch, and Discovery Dynamics
This layer explains how raw telemetry becomes new knowledge structures, where geometry first yields corridors → rivers → terraces.

### 7. Pattern Donation Tensor (Π)
**Formal definition**
\[
\Pi_{i\rightarrow j}=\frac{C_i\cdot\Lambda_{ij}\cdot M_i}{D_j+\epsilon}
\]
Where:
- \(C_i\): coherence of source pattern
- \(\Lambda_{ij}\): phase-lock between domains \(i\) and \(j\)
- \(M_i\): evidence maturity of pattern \(i\)
- \(D_j\): dissipation in receiving domain

**Operational meaning**  
Pattern donation occurs when structure discovered in one field helps solve problems in another.

**Human interpretation**  
Π answers: *“Can this idea help solve problems somewhere else?”*

### 8. Branch Emergence Function (B)
**Formal definition**
\[
B=\frac{\sum_{k=1}^{n}\Pi_k}{D+R}
\]
Where:
- \(\Pi_k\): pattern donations
- \(D\): dissipation
- \(R\): rejection pressure from orthodoxy

**Operational meaning**  
Measures probability a new research branch forms.

**Human interpretation**  
B answers: *“Is this idea becoming a new research direction?”*

### 9. Discovery Corridor Vector Field (V)
**Formal definition**
\[
V=\nabla(\Psi)+\nabla(\Pi)
\]
Where:
- \(\Psi\): coherence functional
- \(\Pi\): pattern donation tensor

**Operational meaning**  
Discovery corridors are vector flows indicating where work is likely to produce breakthroughs.

**Human interpretation**  
V answers: *“Where should we explore next?”*

### 10. River Braiding Density (β)
**Formal definition**
\[
\beta=\frac{N_{corridors}\cdot\Lambda}{D + C_{capture}}
\]
Where:
- \(N_{corridors}\): number of discovery corridors
- \(\Lambda\): synchronization between them
- \(C_{capture}\): institutional capture pressure

**Operational meaning**  
Multiple corridors converge and braid into rivers.

**Human interpretation**  
β answers: *“Are many discoveries converging into something bigger?”*

### 11. Terrace Stability Metric (Tₛ)
**Formal definition**
\[
T_s=\frac{\beta\cdot M\cdot T}{C_{capture}+O_{orthodoxy}}
\]
Where:
- \(M\): memory continuity
- \(T\): transparency
- \(O\): orthodoxy pressure

**Operational meaning**  
A terrace forms when a river stabilizes into a discipline/paradigm/worldview.

**Human interpretation**  
Tₛ answers: *“Has this discovery become stable knowledge?”*
