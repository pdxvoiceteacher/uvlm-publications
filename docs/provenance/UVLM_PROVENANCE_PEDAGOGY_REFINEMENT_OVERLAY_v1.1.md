## Adoption decision

The v1 package is adopted as the working provenance curriculum for Echo and UVLM apprentices. Its core doctrine remains unchanged: provenance is lineage, not truth; integrity is not correctness; authenticity is not authority; canonicalization is stable identity, not certainty; audits evaluate but do not own their subjects; human decisions authorize bounded consequences, not universal truth; retention is consent-bounded inspectability, not canon; and provenance must remain useful and proportionate.

## Refinement 1 — Infer the mission from accepted provenance

Infer outcome, rationale, boundaries, and accepted predecessor state from repository history, phase records, direct human corrections, and public evidence. Ask only where evidence is contradictory, materially incomplete, or a new irreversible authority decision is required.

## Refinement 2 — Use an evidence vector, never one legitimacy score

Keep byte integrity, lineage completeness, source independence, epistemic support, authority clarity, privacy and consent posture, and operational usefulness separate.

## Refinement 3 — Admit a HOLD only by consequence

A blocking HOLD requires observed failure; affected invariant, security/privacy property, or authority boundary; plausible consequence; owner; exact exit test; and retirement condition. Otherwise use `PROCEED`, `PROCEED_WITH_DEBT`, or `REPAIR_IN_CURRENT_WORK_UNIT`.

## Refinement 4 — Freeze gates and inherit evidence

Freeze acceptance before implementation. Accepted predecessor evidence remains valid until the changed surface can plausibly affect it. Newly imagined preferences are backlog unless new evidence shows accepted-invariant failure, material harm, unauthorized authority expansion, or source-identity failure.

## Refinement 5 — Require production-path sensitivity

A test supporting a production-path claim must fail when that production path is deliberately disabled. A helper-only green test cannot support a production-entry-point claim.

## Refinement 6 — Preserve dissent and method freedom

Apprentices may challenge false premises, brittle representations, disproportionate validators, and larger-than-necessary methods while preserving the authorized product outcome. Dissent is not permission to stop when implementation remains possible.

## Refinement 7 — Connected failures close together

Connected runtime, schema, verifier, CLI, and test failures close on one branch; do not serialize one coherent closure into avoidable micro-patches.

## Refinement 8 — Separate driver defects from product defects

A validation-driver defect invalidates that evidence segment; it does not establish a product defect. Correct the driver and rerun only evidence that did not execute or cannot be inherited.

## Refinement 9 — Make pedagogy product-real

Apprentices work with real merge-to-local lineage, bounded evidence ZIPs, manifests, checksum closure, false reds, false greens, candidate → audit → posture → decision traces, revocation, and incomplete public product tranches.

## Refinement 10 — Retire controls that stop teaching

After three consecutive public patches with no material mismatch among task, public source, repository tests, hosted evidence, and target-platform evidence, simplify or retire a control unless it protects a sensitive-data, security, live-authority, or irreversible boundary.
