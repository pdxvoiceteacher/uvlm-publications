## 3. The Spiral Cycle of Knowledge

The fundamental sequence is:

corridor → river → terrace → orthodoxy → rupture → corridor

Each step represents a different structural condition in the knowledge system.

### Discovery Corridor

A corridor appears when coherence increases faster than entropy.

Researchers begin exploring a new region of knowledge.

Example: early quantum mechanics.

### Knowledge River

Multiple corridors braid together.

Independent research lines converge.

Example: the unification of electromagnetism.

### Terrace

A terrace forms when the knowledge river stabilizes.

The field gains shared methods, textbooks, and institutions.

Example: classical mechanics after Newton.

### Orthodoxy

Terraces eventually harden.

Institutional structures resist change.

Example: the late Ptolemaic cosmology.

### Rupture

Contradictions accumulate.

Entropy overwhelms coherence.

Example: the transition from classical to quantum physics.

### Corridor Reopening

Plural experimentation resumes.

New discovery corridors appear.

The cycle begins again.
