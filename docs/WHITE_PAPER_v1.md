## Abstract

The Triadic Brain is a computational architecture that models scientific discovery as a dynamical field evolving over a knowledge lattice. Rather than treating knowledge as a static database or a sequence of predictions, the architecture models epistemic structures as attractors within a coherence potential landscape.

The system combines:

- a coherence field equation
- a graph-based PDE evolution engine
- a governed operator grammar
- a telemetry-validated audit system
- and a multi-agent discovery substrate

Together these components form a coherence navigation engine capable of proposing hypotheses, testing them through simulation, synthesizing theories, and detecting paradigm shifts.

The result is a system that models knowledge evolution as a physical process, allowing scientific exploration to occur autonomously while remaining auditable and governance-bounded.
