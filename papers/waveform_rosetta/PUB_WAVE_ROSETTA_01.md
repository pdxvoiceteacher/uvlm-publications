# Waveform Rosetta Controls for Coherence Metrics: Distinguishing Constructive Coherence, Coherent Cancellation, Detuning, Incomplete Cancellation, and Observability Drift

## Abstract

WAVE Gold-Physics is a closed-form synthetic calibration family for CoherenceLattice metrics. It calibrates metric behavior against known waveform behavior rather than against semantic, psychoacoustic, or deployment outcomes. WAVE-00R through WAVE-03R are accepted members of the Gold-Physics family.

The family distinguishes constructive reinforcement, coherent cancellation, detuning drift, incomplete cancellation, and observability degradation. A central result is that high coherence is not equivalent to constructive output: anti-phase waves can be highly coherent while canceling output. This paper does not prove universal ontology, psychoacoustic effects, AI consciousness, TCHES engineering performance, UNI-02 portability, retrosynthesis readiness, deployment authority, or truth certification. It is a methods/preprint draft that requires external peer review.

## 2. Mathematical Background

Consider two sine waves:

```text
x1(t) = A1 sin(2πf1t + φ1)
x2(t) = A2 sin(2πf2t + φ2)
y(t) = x1(t) + x2(t)
```

The phase difference is:

```text
Δφ = φ2 − φ1
interference_polarity = cos(Δφ)
```

The output `y(t)` is constructive at aligned phase, destructive at anti-phase, and in quadrature at approximately ±π/2. The interference polarity `cos(Δφ)` separates constructive and destructive relations:

```text
constructive_potential = Ψ × (1 + cos Δφ) / 2
cancellation_risk = Ψ × (1 − cos Δφ) / 2
```

For unequal anti-phase amplitudes, cancellation is incomplete:

```text
residual_peak_amplitude ≈ |A1 − A2|
residual_rms ≈ |A1 − A2| / sqrt(2)
```

For detuning, the phase difference evolves through time:

```text
d(Δφ)/dt = 2π(f2 − f1)
```

Noise and jitter can degrade observability without proving structural incoherence. In metric terms, the measured trace may raise ΔS and reduce confidence even when the underlying phase structure remains explainable.

## 3. WAVE Family Experiments

### 3.1 WAVE-00R — static phase sweep / constructive, destructive, quadrature

WAVE-00R sweeps phase relation across constructive, destructive, and quadrature cases. The allowed claim is that the system can classify phase relations and distinguish constructive from destructive interference. The blocked overclaim is that a universal ontology, psychoacoustic effect, or AI cognition claim would be outside scope.

### 3.2 WAVE-01R — detuning / beat dynamics

WAVE-01R introduces slight frequency mismatch. The allowed claim is that slight frequency mismatch creates recurrent rephasing and boundary events. The blocked overclaim is that beat dynamics prove human entrainment, symbolic-sonic effects, or universal portability.

### 3.3 WAVE-02R — unequal amplitude / incomplete cancellation

WAVE-02R evaluates anti-phase waves with unequal amplitudes. The allowed claim is that a destructive relation can remain coherent even when residual output is nonzero. The blocked overclaim is that a nonzero residual means the system has found a good or safe answer.

### 3.4 WAVE-03R — noise / jitter / observability

WAVE-03R introduces noise and jitter. The allowed claim is that noise and jitter degrade observability and raise ΔS without proving structural incoherence. The blocked overclaim is that noise alone proves incoherence or that observability loss establishes deployment readiness.
